Seven Days, Seven Visualisations is a series of data experiments created by Vizzuality to celebrate and showcase the world’s protected areas. Experts from all over the world are coming together in Sydney for one week at the World Parks Congress, a once-in-a-decade event that sets the agenda for protecting the world’s most valuable places.

Starting Wednesday 12th November (the opening day of the congress), we will release a different visualisation every day to illustrate the various aspects that make the world’s protected areas so special. We have combined open data from the World Database on Protected Areas, the IUCN Red List and many other sources to create each one. Come back every day at 8:30am AEDT time (timezone) to see the latest visualisation. For more information contact contact@vizzuality.com

Visualization 1: The Expansion of Protected Areas

This visualization takes data on the status date and location of every protected area in the world from the World Database on Protected Areas. Using Odyssey.js we turned this into an illustration of the expansion in protected areas over time, especially in the last 50 years since the first World Parks Congress in 1962.

Data from: ProtectedPlanet.net
